What is ifni?

Lake Ifni is located in the High Atlas Mountains at 2,500 meters above sea level. Its history dates back 145 million years. It is 900 meters by 400 meters and its depth is unknown. Considered a natural water reservoir, it feeds 10 villages with 300 liters per second year-round. Myths claim an ancient city with 66 saints lies at the bottom, but scientists conclude it is an extinct volcanic crater that accumulated water long ago. The map

Road Map

.01

The Start From Marrakesh

After you enjoy the atmosphere of Marrakesh, our agency will pick you up from your Hotel/Riad and the trip will begin to the High Atlas mountains

.02

Get to the Tifnout region

As we drive 191 km towards the Tifnout area, we will take time to savor the beauty of nature, including a must-see stop at Tadart. We will also break for meals and rest, ensuring we are refreshed for the journey ahead.

.03

Night at the cozy inn

Upon our arrival at the charming village of Amsouzart, we will settle in at the village inn. Following a refreshing cup of mint tea, we will embark on a tour of the local creek and orchards, gaining insight into the fascinating Amazigh culture.

.04

Reach the destination

On the following morning, we will hop in our vehicles and make a 15-minute drive up to the Andous area, where we will begin our exhilarating 1-hour (4km) mountain trek to the breathtaking Lake Ifni. Once we arrive, we will relish the stunning scenery and indulge in a delicious lunch while overlooking the serene lake

.05

Discover more on your way back

As our journey draws to a close, we will depart using an alternate route to Marrakesh, passing through the captivating Asni region and Moulay Brahim. This will provide us with an opportunity to explore and appreciate other areas on our return trip.
